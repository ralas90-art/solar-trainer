## Trainer Script: Solar Landscape & Your Market (20 Minutes)

*(Trainer Notes: Read this word-for-word or use as a strict outline. Project confidence. Eye contact is critical.)*

"Welcome to Day 1. By the end of this 7-day Accelerator, you will be field-ready to run your own appointments and close your own deals. Today, we start with the foundation. You cannot sell a solution if you don't understand the problem. 

The problem is the modern utility grid. It’s antiquated, fragile, and a monopoly that bleeds homeowners dry. For the last 100 years, homeowners have rented their power—meaning they pay for electricity, but they never build equity in their energy. They are subject to unpredictable rate hikes. 

[PAUSE] *Take a look at the utility bill example in your workbook. Notice the delivery charges versus the generation charges.*

Every time the utility company builds a new power plant or runs new lines, they pass that cost to the consumer. And they are legally allowed to do so. That is the 'Solar Landscape' we operate in. We are not selling panels. We are selling independence and financial predictability. 

Now, let's talk about our specific market. In our state, homeowners have access to incredible incentives—like Net Metering, or NEM. Net Metering is simply rollover minutes for your power. During the day, your panels produce more than your home uses. That excess spins the meter backward, banking credits with the utility. At night, when the sun goes down, you use those credits. 

[PAUSE] *Who can give me a quick summary of Net Metering based on what I just said? (Wait for a trainee to respond.)*

Excellent. In addition to Net Metering, we have the Federal Investment Tax Credit, the ITC. Right now, the federal government covers 30% of the gross cost of the system as a tax credit. Let me be brilliantly clear on this point: this is a tax credit, not a rebate check. It offsets their tax liability. 

[LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED: Confirm state-specific exact phrasing for tax credit explanations to comply with FTC and consumer protection guidelines. Do not guarantee a tax return.]

Our role as Consultants is not to 'pitch' a product. It is to educate the homeowner on how their current setup is a financial liability, and how we can swap their fluctuating utility rent for a fixed, owned asset that increases their home value. 

Any questions so far? 

[PAUSE] *Answer any initial questions on the grid vs. solar.*

Let's dive into the Trainee Workbook to solidify these concepts."

### Assessment Answer Key & Rationale

1. **B** - *Rationale:* The grid is a monopoly. Homeowners are forced to rent power at rates that constantly rise, which is the core problem we solve.
2. **B** - *Rationale:* People don't buy panels; they buy the financial predictability and independence that panels provide.
3. **C** - *Rationale:* "Rollover minutes" is the most intuitive way for customers to understand how Net Metering stores their value on the grid.
4. **B** - *Rationale:* Excess daytime power is sent to the grid, banking credits for later use.
5. **C** - *Rationale:* The Investment Tax Credit is the federal incentive program for solar.
6. **C** - *Rationale:* The current rate is 30% under the Inflation Reduction Act.
7. **C** - *Rationale:* It is crucial for compliance that reps understand it is a tax liability offset, not a guaranteed cash check or an upfront discount.
8. **B** - *Rationale:* Framing solar as "Redirected Spend" removes the fear of taking on a new debt. It is money they are already spending.
9. **B** - *Rationale:* You must establish the pain (fluctuating, endless utility rent) before presenting the cure (fixed, owned solar asset).
10. **Scenario Output Expected:** The trainee should acknowledge the $150 amount, but pivot to the fact that $150 is "rent" that goes up every year and never builds equity. A good answer will mention that a fixed solar payment replaces that rent, protecting them from future rate hikes while building an asset.
