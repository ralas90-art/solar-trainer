# Day 1: Solar Sales Accelerator Program

**Mission:** Remove administrative friction, build identity, create excitement
**Target Audience:** New solar sales reps (0-30 days experience)
**Goal:** Get reps field-ready and closing deals within 7 days
**Training Philosophy:** Consultant mindset, integrity-first, performance-driven

---

## 1. TRAINER FACILITATION GUIDE

### Module 1: Welcome & Vision Casting (15 min)
**Objective:** Set the tone, establish high expectations, and inspire the new hires.

**Trainer Dialogue (Word-for-Word):**
"Welcome to Day 1 of the Solar Sales Accelerator. Look around the room. You are here because we saw potential in you to become a top-tier Solar Consultant. By Day 7, you won’t just know how solar works; you will be in the field, confidently leading appointments, and guiding homeowners toward financial independence.

"This industry is not about throwing panels on a roof. We are in the business of replacing unpredictable, rising utility rents with a stable, owned asset. Over the next seven days, we will systematically build your skills. If you commit to the process, trust the systems, and lead with integrity, this career will change your life. 

"Are you ready to build something that lasts?"
*(Wait for a collective "Yes!" or head nods.)*

**Discussion Prompt:** "Why did you choose solar sales?" *(Take 2-3 quick answers).*

---

### Module 2: Administrative Onboarding (45-60 min)
**Objective:** Eliminate operational friction so reps can focus entirely on learning.

**Trainer Dialogue:**
"Before we talk strategy, we must handle logistics. A true professional has their tools sharp and ready. Today, we are setting up your CRM access, your payroll documentation, and installing the apps you need for canvassing and communication."

**Checklist Walkthrough:**
*(Guide trainees through the Onboarding Checklist on their devices.)*
- CRM login verified.
- Communication platform (e.g., Slack/Teams) installed.
- HR portal completed (W-4, I-9, Direct Deposit).

---

### Module 3: Company Culture & Expectations (20 min)
**Objective:** Embed the core values and define the standard of excellence.

**Trainer Dialogue:**
"Culture isn't a poster on the wall. It’s what we tolerate and what we celebrate. At this company, our first metric is Integrity. 

"[COMPLIANCE WARNING] Let me be very clear: We do not use deceptive marketing. We do not promise 'Free Solar.' We promise 'Zero Down' ownership. We do not guarantee tax return checks. We explain tax liability offsets. If you lead with deception, you will not survive here. If you lead with education, you will thrive."

**Coaching Question:** "Why do you think the phrase 'Free Solar' is actually harmful to our industry?"
**Suggested Answer:** "It sets false expectations, sounds like a scam, and damages trust immediately."

---

### Module 4: Solar Industry Overview (20 min)
**Objective:** Provide historical context for why the solar transition is inevitable.

**Trainer Dialogue:**
"The grid was designed over a century ago. It relies on burning centralized fuel and pushing it hundreds of miles through fragile wires. What happens? Prices go up, and the grid fails. 

"Enter Distributed Generation—Solar. Every home becomes its own power plant. You aren’t just selling an appliance; you are upgrading the entire country's infrastructure. Homeowners are swapping an unpredictable liability, their utility bill, for a fixed, predictable asset."

---

### Module 5: Solar Technology Fundamentals (25 min)
**Objective:** Simplify the physics of solar energy generation.

**Trainer Dialogue:**
"You don’t need an engineering degree, but you must know the basics. 
1. **Panels:** Sunlight hits the silicon cells, creating Direct Current (DC) power.
2. **Inverter:** The home runs on Alternating Current (AC). The inverter flips DC to AC.
3. **Net Metering:** This is the most important concept. Think of it like 'rollover minutes' for cell phones. During the day, you make more power than you use. The excess goes to the grid, spinning the meter backward. At night, you pull those credits back."

---

### Module 6: Identity Shift - Consultant vs Salesperson (20 min)
**Objective:** Reprogram the approach from "pitching" to "advising."

**Trainer Dialogue:**
"A 'Salesperson' tries to convince a homeowner to buy something they don't want. A 'Consultant' uncovers a problem the homeowner didn't fully realize they had, and offers a logical solution. 

"A salesperson focuses on the price of the panels. A consultant compares the lifetime cost of renting power from the utility versus the flat cost of owning solar. Which one do you want to be?"

---

### Module 7: Utility Bill Mastery (Introduction) (25 min)
**Objective:** Teach reps to read the utility bill like a doctor reads an X-ray.

**Trainer Dialogue:**
"The utility bill is your greatest weapon. It’s the proof of pain. Look at the sample bill in your workbook. Notice the 'Generation' charge versus the 'Delivery' charge. Notice the 'Public Purpose Programs' fees. They are paying for a service they don’t control. 

"Your job is to show them the math. If their bill is $200 a month, and energy rates historically rise 6% a year, what does that cost them over 25 years? Ask them that question."

---

### Module 8: Day 1 Wrap-Up & Preview (15 min)
**Objective:** Solidify the day's learning and build anticipation for Day 2.

**Trainer transition:** "Today, we built the foundation. You know why we are here, how the tech works, and the mindset required. Tomorrow, we get into the psychology of the buyer. We will cover BOLT profiling and financial qualification. Do your homework tonight, get a good night's sleep, and come ready to work."

## 4. ROLE-PLAY SCENARIOS

### Scenario 1: The "What is Net Metering?" Explanation
**Setup:** You are sitting at the kitchen table. The homeowner is confused about what happens to the solar power at night.
**Character Profile:** "Skeptical Steve" - 55-year-old accountant. Needs logic and simple analogies. Does not want to buy batteries.
**Trainee Task:** Explain Net Metering seamlessly without using overly technical jargon.

**Success Criteria (Rubric):**
- [ ] Avoids overly technical terms (e.g., bi-directional, sine waves).
- [ ] Uses the "Rollover Minutes" or "Banking" analogy.
- [ ] Confirms the homeowner's understanding before moving on.

**Coaching Feedback Template:**
- **What worked:** You sounded confident and maintained good eye contact.
- **Area to improve:** You used the term "kilowatt-hour" before defining it. Stick to the simple "rollover minutes" analogy for the first explanation.

## 5. ASSESSMENT MATERIALS

### Day 1 Knowledge Quiz 

**1. What is the fundamental problem with the current utility grid for homeowners?**
a) Not enough power is generated.
b) Homeowners are forced to rent unpredictable power forever.
c) Solar panels are too heavy.
d) It relies purely on the sun.

**2. Which of the following is an acceptable, compliant way to describe a zero-down loan?**
a) "You will get free solar for your home."
b) "The government pays for everything."
c) "This is a Zero-Down ownership program where we redirect your current utility spending."
d) "You will never pay an electric bill again."

**3. What does an Inverter do?**
a) Stores energy for the night.
b) Converts DC power from the panels into usable AC power for the home.
c) Measures the wind speed.
d) Connects directly to the internet.

**4. How should you explain Net Metering to a homeowner?**
a) A complicated tax algorithm based on grid usage.
b) Rollover minutes for your power; banking daytime excess for nighttime use.
c) Selling power to your neighbors at a premium.
d) Disconnecting entirely from the grid.

**5. What is the difference between a Salesperson and a Consultant?**
a) A Salesperson pushes products; a Consultant uncovers problems and provides logical solutions.
b) A Consultant talks louder.
c) A Salesperson uses iPads; a Consultant uses paper.
d) There is no difference.

### Answer Key with Explanations
1. **B.** The utility is a monopoly where the homeowner builds no equity and is subject to endless rate hikes.
2. **C.** [COMPLIANCE WARNING] "Free solar" is deceptive. "Zero-Down" accurately describes the financing vehicle without misleading the customer.
3. **B.** Panels generate DC; homes use AC. The inverter is the bridge.
4. **B.** Simplicity wins. "Rollover minutes" is universally understood.
5. **A.** Our culture depends on high-level consultative advising, not high-pressure pitching.
