## 2. Evaluation & Analysis

### A. Is the Offer Clear?
*   **Yes.** The transition from standard seat-based pricing ($99/user/month + $1,500 setup fee) to the **Founding Partner Pilot** ($499/month flat fee for the whole office, waived setup fees, and direct feedback loops) is structured cleanly.
*   *Action Item:* Ensure the hosts emphasize that this pilot rate is locked in for **6 months** and is strictly capped at **10 companies** to build urgency.

### B. Is the Product Positioning Strong?
*   **Yes.** SeptiVolt is positioned as an **AI Solar Sales Training OS** rather than a generic roleplay tool. It is framed as an operational infrastructure that standardizes training, certifies field readiness, and frees up management time.
*   *Action Item:* Keep the language focused on the "flight simulator for solar reps" analogy. This makes it easy for sales leaders to visualize the product's function immediately.

### C. Are the Buyer Pain Points Obvious?
*   **Yes.** The pitch successfully attacks three core solar agency bottlenecks:
    1.  **Lead Burn Rate:** Reps practicing on live, expensive leads.
    2.  **Manager Burnout:** Leaders wasting valuable closer hours running basic roleplays.
    3.  **High Turnover:** Representatives quitting in their first 30 days due to a lack of field confidence.

### D. Is the Pilot Offer Compelling?
*   **Yes.** The combination of waived setup fees, flat-rate pricing, white-glove onboarding, and direct roadmap input provides massive value. The addition of the **30-day money-back guarantee** removes the risk for owners.

### E. Identifying Gaps / Confusing Areas
*   **Enterprise Readiness:** While we highlight security (Row-Level Security) and database optimization (v2.4.2 login performance overrides), we must remain honest about integration timelines. We support custom webhooks today and are actively developing native GHL CRM sync tools. We should position ourselves as *pilot-ready and actively stabilizing* rather than having a finished, legacy enterprise platform.
*   **Industry Lingo:** Watch for generic "business training" talk. Ensure all roleplays and messaging focus on solar-specific terms (e.g., Net Metering, NEM 3.0, ITC Tax Credits, Utility Bill Autopsy, D2D door interrupts, and EPC integrations).
